Pre-Renovation — Before Demo Starts

Must be completed before any construction begins. Some have 2–4 week lead times — start them in parallel with team assembly.

🟠 Pre-Reno
Pre-1978 construction: This house predates 1978. Before any demo, sanding, cutting, or drilling into existing surfaces, assume lead paint and possibly asbestos until tested. Contractors must follow EPA RRP rules. Asbestos testing must happen before demo day — not on the same day.
Testing — Must Happen Before Demo
Asbestos testing — kitchen and bathrooms
Pre-RenoHire Out
Already in the budget. In a 1960s home, asbestos is most commonly found in: floor tile adhesive (mastic), drywall joint compound, pipe insulation around boiler lines, and ceiling texture. A certified inspector takes samples and sends to a lab — results in 2–5 business days. If found, abatement before demo. If not found, proceed. Do not skip this step thinking you'll "deal with it if it comes up" — disturbing asbestos without proper handling is both a health hazard and a legal liability.
⚡ Must complete before any demo work

How to find: Search "asbestos testing Denver certified inspector" or "Colorado asbestos inspector CDPHE." Inspector must be certified by Colorado CDPHE. Ask about turnaround time — some labs offer 24-hour rush if you need results faster.

Appliance selection — shop during design, not before
Decide During Design
All existing upper kitchen appliances are being replaced. Do not buy appliances before design is underway — appliance dimensions (especially refrigerator, range, and range hood) directly affect cabinet layout. Buying first and designing around them wastes money on returns or creates awkward gaps. The right sequence: (1) Early in design, decide appliance types and rough sizes so the designer can plan openings — particularly fridge width (30" vs 33" vs 36"), cooktop configuration, and whether you want a built-in or freestanding fridge. (2) Mid-design, select specific models so exact dimensions are locked in before cabinets are ordered. (3) Order appliances for delivery after construction is complete — appliances in an active construction zone get damaged. Key items to select: refrigerator, dishwasher, range hood (must be exterior-vented, not recirculating — affects cabinet layout above), and any undercounter or specialty appliances. Induction cooktop is already decided. Note: the upstairs dishwasher (Kenmore, 2013) was not operated during inspection due to the disposal leak — run it once before demo to confirm function. It's being replaced regardless, but knowing its condition costs nothing.
